Example

In this example we will be acting as a company who buys and sells bulldozers. So let's say that we have past data about the selling price of bulldozers in an excel(or csv) sheet along with various other factors and features of the bull dozer as given in the download link :
Training Data
Our target variable here is the sale price and all the other columns are the factors that affect it. So I will submit it as my training data.

The next sheet is the prediction data sheet given here :
Prediction Data
It contains features and factors of bulldozers whose price is not known by us.
The important thing to note here is that the columns (features and factors) of both prediction data and the training data are same. The only difference is that training data contains SalePrice and the prediction data does not.
We also have a date column in the columns by the name of "saledate" but it is not necessary for your data to have so (although it is recommended).

So now we will go on to fill the form which is there in the link provided at the end of this text. So in the form we will fill the fields as :
1. Target Variable - SalePrice (It must exactly match the name of the corresponding column in the sheet)
2. Email Id - Fill your email id
3. Training data - Upload the training data
4. Prediction Data - Upload the prediction data
5. Date Column (OPTIONAL)- Our data does contain one by the name of 'saledate' so fill 'saledate' here.

You will receive the prediction in your mail(as a csv file) in the order in which you had given your prediction data. It will also contain the feature importance graph which shows which of your columns impact the target variable the most and in what order.


Please make sure that you do not remove the column titles while submitting and that the data should all be in one sheet ( one each for training and prediction). To further understand the format check out the document given at the links put up in the example above.


This example was taken from the Kaggle 'Blue Book for Bulldozers' dataset.
